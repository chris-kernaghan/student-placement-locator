Our Placement Co-Ordinator

Our Placement Co-ordinator is Christine Costello. Christine travels out to placements to complete checks and insurance details. She also visits you out on your placements to get an understanding of how you are finding the work. If you have a placement in mind and it cannot be found here or for more infromation please contact Christine by using the contact button at the top of the page.

Why is placement important?

There are a wide range of placement available whether you are interested in domestic, agricultural, veterinary, physiotherapy, agriculture, conservation and many more. Work placement is so important to the course as you will gain valuable skills in an area in with you find interesting and may want to pursue a career. This is fantastic for your CV and future job prospects!

What does placement involve?

Your work placement will take place on one day per week. This will be a day out of college to carry this out. It is up to you to find and contact a placement and they will arrange with you how long your working day will be and what roles and responsibilities you may take on while you are there. You are required to complete up to 150 hours for Level 3 Animal Management per year.
